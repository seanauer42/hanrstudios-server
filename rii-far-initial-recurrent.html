RII/FAR Initial/Recurrent

  1. A discrepany has been upgraded to an RII. It is necessary for an RII Inspector to ensurer a pressure/leak check is performed if the maintenance manual calls for it to be done
    True
  2. FAR Part 121 governs what?
    Operating requirements: Domestic Flag and supplemental Operators
  3. Who can perform the "EQI" Inspection?
    ETOPS QC Inspector
  4. All steps of an AMM task/sub-task that have a flashing red RII indicator require buyback
    False. Only the items/actions identified within Table 1 of GMM 7-1-100 require RII buyback
  5. When will a maintenance action be classified as a Required Inspection Item?
    By maintenance before the work begins
  6. All RII's must have 2 signatures, one from a properly certified mechanic who performed the work, and one authorized to buy-back the task
    True
  7. Who can perform the "EQM" Inspection?
    ETOPS AMT
  8. As an RII Inspector you may designate a maintenance task as a required inspection item
    False
  9. Who can perform the "I" Inspection?
    Aircraft Quality Control Inspector
  10. As an RII Inspector designee having authorization to perform a buy-back on a 767 aircraft is also authorized to perform buy-backs on a 757 aircraft
    False
  11. As an aircraft mechanic, you assume the position of an RII inspector designee. You are now under the control and supervision of the Chief Inspector while performing such duties
    True
  12. Which part and section specifically state that no person may perform a required inspection if he performed the item of work required to be inspected?
    121.371
  13. The following scenario occurs. You (The RII) have been contacted for an RII installation. You have complied with the installation inspection...
    Request the AMT return to the corrective action screen and review the text for appropriate steps and references, then give RII approval via the password screen
  14. How often must FAR and RII recurrent training be completed in order to remain current?
    Annually
  15. The decision of any person authorized to perfrom RII buy-back must onot be countermanded by any person except the director of maintenance
    False
  16. As an AMT while replacing an aileron cable pully you find that with the use of cable blocks it can be replaced without slacking the cables, therefore no RII is required
    False, because it is still a breach of system integrity, and pulley installation still needs checked along with any test/checks per the AMM
  17. You have been issued a workcard containing several steps as follows: Who is responsible for these tasks
    The AMT signs for the "M" blocks, a qualified member of Aircraft Quality Control Department signs the "I", and the RII signs off the "RII" block
  18. you are accomplishing a task which requires the replacement of a cargo door latching/locking actuator and no adjustment was required. Per GMM 7-1-100 Table 1 is an RII buyback required?
    Yes, The installation, rigging, and test require RII buyback
  19. Who can perform the "M" Inspection?
    AMT
  20. The Temporary RII Authorizationis for both Scheduled and Unscheduled maintenance events.
    False
  21. Per GMM 7-1-100, only QC management and QC Inspectors are authorized to designate any maintenance task as RII
    True
  22. How is the maintenance action denoted as an RII on the Aircraft Maintenance Log (AML) page?
    Denoted by checking the "YES/NO" block marked RII for that discrepancy
  23. When an RII authorized AMT is working a repair, via an Engineering Authorization (EA), FACTing the EAML does not require a RII sign-off because the RII sign-offs are within the EA
    False, An EA does not havee the RII requirements pre-defined and must be determined before work begins, and the FACTing must have an RII buy-back from a QC Inspector or RII Authorized AMT
  24. Can an RII authorized AMT accomplish work within a FedEx Express Work instruction Card, or an Engineering Order (EO), and act as the RII for separate work steps/items?
    Yes, provided that the RII authorized AMT, accomplishing the buy-back, did not do any part of the work step/item being bought-back
  25. An engine change has been accomplished per a FedEx Express Work Instruction Card. When FACTing out the work release item in the EAML, does the fact-out require an RII buy-back?
    No, because a FedEx Express Work Instruction Card has the skill levels and RII requirements identified for individual steps/items
  26. FedEx Express Engineering Order Work Instruction Cards (EOs) and Work Instruction Cards have skill levels and RII requirements identified
    True
  27. An aileron actuator is being removed and replaced per an AMM reference. Can an RII authorized AMT accomplish sub tasks/items with the repair and also buy-back other sub-tasks/items within the same repair?
    No, because the RII requirements must be determines for each sub-task/item, a QC Inspector or RII authorized AMT who is not accomplishing the repair must accomplish the buy-back
  28. You are accomplishing a maintenance task of an RII identified system/component. As part of the troubleshooting the Aircraft Maintenance Manual requires a system test to be accomplished. Is the system test required to have an RII buyback?
    No, because GMM 7-1-100 Table 1 states: Rig/Adjust/Test: Test is RII only after the Rigging, Adjustment, or Installation of an RII identified item has been accomplished
